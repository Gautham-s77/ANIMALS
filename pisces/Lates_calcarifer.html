Lates calcarifer (Bloch, 1790)

Environment: Marine; freshwater; brackish; demersal; catadromous
Distribution: Indo-West Pacific,China, Taiwan and southern Japan and northern Australia.
Depth range: 10-40m
Description: Dorsal spines (total): 7 - 9; Dorsal soft rays (total): 10-11; Anal spines: 3; Anal soft rays: 7 - 8. Body elongate; mouth large, slightly oblique, upper jaw extending behind the eye. Caudal fin rounded.
Biology: Found in coastal waters, estuaries and lagoons, A diadromous fish, inhabiting rivers before returning to the estuaries to spawn. A protandrous hermaphrodite.
Feeding Biology: fishes and crustaceans
Remarks: Most important commercial fish and one of the most popular angling species
