Sillago sihama ( Forsskal,1775)

Environment: Marine; brackish; reef-associated; amphidromous
Distribution: Indo-West Pacific: southern Red Sea South Africa to Japan and south to Australia
Depth range: 0-60m
Description: Dorsal spines (total): 11 - 13; Dorsal soft rays (total): 20-23; Anal spines: 2; Anal soft rays: 18 - 23. Swim bladder with two anterior and two posterior extensions.
Biology: Common along beaches, sandbars, mangrove creeks and estuaries
Feeding Biology: polychaete worms, small prawns (Penaeus), shrimps and amphipods
Remarks: Rarely captured by prawn trawling vessels. Delicious and commercially important species
